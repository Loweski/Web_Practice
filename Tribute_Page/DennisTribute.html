Dennis Ritchie

Father of the "C" Programming Language

Timeline of Dennis Ritchie's Life:

  • 1941-Borned in Bronxville, New York.
  • 1967-Started working at the Bell Labs Computing Sciences Research Center
  • 1968-Presented his thesis "Program Structure and Computational Complexity" at Harvard but never officially given his PhD
  • 1970-Along with Ken Thompson and Brian Kernighan, created Unix. Led to the development of B by Thompson which later replaced by C, developed my Ritchie
  • 1983-Received the Turing Award
  • 1990-Received IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal
  • 1990s-Transferred to Lucent Technologies
  • 1997-Made a fellow of the Computer History Museum
  • 1999-Received the National Medal of Technology of 1998 from President Bill Clinton
  • 2005-Received an achievement award from Industrial Research Institute
  • 2007-Retired as head of System Software Research Department
  • 2011-Received the Japan Prize for Information and Commnications
  • Oct. 12, 2011-Passed away at the age of 70

"Ritchie was under the radar. His name was not a household name at all, but... if you had a microscope and could look in a computer, you'd see his work everywhere inside."

-Computer Historian Paul E. Ceruzzi
